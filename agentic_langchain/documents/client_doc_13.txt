CLIENT PROFILE - GLOBAL COMMODITIES TRADING GROUP

Company Overview:
Global Commodities Trading Group (GCTG) is an international commodity trading firm headquartered in Singapore with regional offices in London, Houston, and Dubai. Founded in 2015, the company specializes in trading physical and derivative contracts for energy, metals, and agricultural commodities.

Primary Business Activities:
GCTG engages in the professional trading of primary and secondary commodities across multiple markets:

Energy Commodities:
- Crude oil and refined petroleum products
- Natural gas and LNG
- Electricity futures and physical delivery contracts
- Petrochemical products including ethylene and propylene

Metals Trading:
- Precious metals: gold, silver, platinum, palladium
- Industrial metals: copper, aluminum, zinc, nickel
- Steel and iron ore
- Rare earth minerals

Agricultural Commodities:
- Soft commodities: coffee, cocoa, sugar, cotton
- Grains: wheat, corn, soybeans
- Vegetable oils and livestock products

Trading Model:
The company operates as a direct market participant, executing trades for its own account and on behalf of institutional clients. GCTG takes physical delivery of commodities and manages logistics, storage, and transportation. We do not engage in upstream extraction/mining operations or downstream retail consumer sales (B2C).

Financial Performance:
Annual Trading Volume: $2.8 billion
Revenue: $145 million
Income Sources:
- Energy commodity trading: 45% ($65.2M)
- Metals trading: 35% ($50.8M)
- Agricultural trading: 20% ($29M)

Direct engagement in commodity trading represents more than 95% of total company income. The remaining 5% comes from advisory services and market research provided to industrial clients.

Management:
James Richardson serves as CEO with direct oversight of all trading operations. The firm employs 85 professional traders and risk managers. All traders hold relevant certifications and licenses required for commodity market participation.

Risk Management:
GCTG maintains comprehensive risk management protocols including:
- Daily position limits and mark-to-market valuations
- Hedging strategies using futures and options
- Credit risk assessment for counterparties
- Insurance coverage for physical commodity holdings

The company maintains trading relationships with major commodity exchanges including NYMEX, LME, ICE, and CME. We hold required exchange memberships and regulatory approvals for commodity trading activities.

Compliance:
GCTG operates under regulatory oversight from the Monetary Authority of Singapore and maintains registration with the CFTC for US commodity trading activities. We comply with all applicable financial crime prevention regulations including AML/KYC procedures.
